AUDITPOL SECURITY ENGINEERING

TAS commercial licensing guide

Designed for online fulfilment and fully offline restricted environments.

Core principle

A customer’s audit evidence is never used to issue or validate a licence. Licensing handles product identity, edition, machine binding, dates and update entitlement only.

Activation lifecycle

1
Entitlement created
Purchase or manual vendor approval records Community, Professional or Consultant rights.
2
Activation request produced locally
TAS generates a request containing product version, edition request, installation identifier and machine fingerprint.
3
Private signing boundary
The vendor service verifies entitlement and signs the licence. Private signing keys never enter browser code or customer packages.
4
Licence imported
The customer imports the returned .taslic. TAS verifies signature, product, edition, machine binding and dates locally.
5
Downloads and updates
Authenticated members receive the correct protected package. Update entitlement controls access to newer releases.
6
Perpetual-use boundary
When the update period expires, the purchased installed Professional version continues to work. New releases require renewal.

Offline environments

The request and response can move through an approved transfer process. TAS does not require permanent internet access after the signed licence is imported.

The current browser buttons are UAT demonstrations only. They do not constitute production cryptographic signing or payment fulfilment.

Device replacement and reset

A controlled reset invalidates or retires the old activation before issuing a replacement. Manual vendor override remains available for exceptional customer support, with an audit record.
